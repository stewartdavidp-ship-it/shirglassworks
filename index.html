How We Work

From Raw Material to Art

Every piece begins with sand, soda, and lime — transformed through heat, skill, and creative vision.

Furnace Glass

Molten glass gathered from a 2,100°F furnace is shaped on the blowpipe using centuries-old techniques — gravity, breath, and hand tools.

Flamework

Delicate details and intricate forms created using a torch — from anatomical hearts to sea creatures — all crafted from glass rods and tubing.

Fusing & Coldwork

Layers of glass combined in a kiln, then refined through cutting, grinding, and polishing — adding texture and precision to every piece.
